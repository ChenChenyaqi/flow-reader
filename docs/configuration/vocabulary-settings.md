# Vocabulary Settings

Configure how FlowReader handles vocabulary based on your proficiency level.

## Vocabulary Levels

Choose the level that best matches your current vocabulary:

### 500 Words (A1 - Beginner)

For those just starting with English. Focuses on the most common words.

### 1000 Words (A2 - Elementary)

For basic conversational ability. Includes everyday vocabulary.

### 2000 Words (B1 - Intermediate) **[Default]**

For comfortable everyday reading. Covers most common texts.

### 3000 Words (B2 - Upper Intermediate)

For academic and professional reading. Includes more advanced vocabulary.

### 5000 Words (C1 - Advanced)

For complex texts and nuanced understanding. Sophisticated vocabulary.

### 8000 Words (C2 - Proficient)

Near-native vocabulary level. Only the most advanced words are highlighted.

## Setting Your Level

1. Open FlowReader Settings
2. Go to the **Vocabulary** tab
3. Select your level from the dropdown
4. Your choice is automatically saved

## Managing Known Words

As you learn words, you can mark them as **"Known"** to:

- Filter them from future analyses
- Focus on new vocabulary
- Track your progress

### Bulk Operations

You can:

- Mark multiple words as "Known" at once
- Export your known words list
- Reset your vocabulary history

## Recommendations

- Start with a lower level if you want to see more vocabulary help
- Adjust upward as you learn more words
- Be honest about your level - it's okay to see familiar words as reminders
- Regular marking of known words improves the filtering quality
